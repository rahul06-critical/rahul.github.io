Munnar

Munnar is one of the most famous hill stations in India. It is located in the Western Ghats and is known for its vast tea plantations, cool climate, and scenic mountain views.

  • Tea gardens
  • Trekking and hiking
  • Wildlife photography
  • Tea Museum

The fresh air and peaceful atmosphere make it a perfect place for nature lovers.

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Alleppey is known as the “Venice of the East.” It is famous for its backwaters, lagoons, and houseboat cruises.

Visitors can stay in traditional houseboats that move slowly through the calm waterways while enjoying beautiful views of villages, coconut trees, and rice fields.

Backwater tourism is one of the most unique experiences in Kerala.

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is a famous beach destination located near Thiruvananthapuram.

  • Golden sandy beaches
  • Lighthouse view
  • Swimming and surfing
  • Water sports

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

The sanctuary is home to many animals such as:

  • Elephants
  • Tigers
  • Deer
  • Wild boars
  • Birds

Athirappilly Waterfalls

Athirappilly Waterfalls is the largest waterfall in Kerala and is often called the “Niagara of India.”
